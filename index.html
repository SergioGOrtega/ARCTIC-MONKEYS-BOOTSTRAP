ARCTIC MONKEYS

- "I Bet You Look Good on the Dancefloor"

Alex Turner

Alex Turner was born in 1986, in Sheffield, England. Alex is the son of two teachers, who from a very young age taught him to speak German and introduced him to music. In 2001, they gave him his first guitar.

Their first major success came with their debut album, "Whatever People Say I Am, That's What I'm Not," released in 2006. The most successful song from that album was "I Bet You Look Good on the Dancefloor," which reached number one on the UK singles charts. This album received critical acclaim and helped establish the band as one of the foremost in the British music scene.

Jamie Cook

Jamie Robert Cook is the lead guitarist of Arctic Monkeys, also contributing backing vocals. Jamie Cook and Alex Turner are neighbors in High Green. Similar to Alex Turner, Cook asked for a musical instrument as a Christmas present in 2001.

Nick O'Malley

After Andy Nicholson announced that he wouldn't be touring North America with the band, O'Malley, a friend of the band and the bassist for The Dodgems, was brought in as a temporary replacement. His first appearance with the band was on May 25, 2006, during a secret concert at the Old Blue Last in East London. The venue, with a capacity of 120 people, was seen as an opportunity to audition O'Malley before the band's first concert in Vancouver on May 27, 2006.

Matt Helders

According to Q magazine's list of the top 10 drummers in history, Helders ranks as the 9th best drummer of all time, ahead of Reni from The Stone Roses. Helders mentioned he ended up as the drummer because "it was the only thing left. When we started the band, none of us knew how to play anything. We figured it out. The others already had guitars, and I bought a drum kit after a while." However, Helders has acknowledged the influence of rap music on the band.
